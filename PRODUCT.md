# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Local-first AI builders and product engineers use Smalltalk while testing whether desktop capture evidence is accurate enough to explain recent work. They are usually in an implementation or research flow and need to inspect what was captured without trusting a black box.

## Product Purpose

Smalltalk captures local screen evidence so the user can later answer what they were doing, why they moved away, what evidence mattered, and where to resume. Success means each resume cue can be justified with local frames, events, text sources, transitions, and app context.

## Brand Personality

Forensic calm, notebook-readable, trustworthy. The interface should feel precise and inspectable without becoming cold or hostile.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS dashboards, decorative metrics, marketing-card layouts, and vague analytics surfaces. Avoid making capture feel like a surveillance archive or a raw database browser.

## Design Principles

- Show evidence before interpretation.
- Make capture quality falsifiable at frame level.
- Keep privacy and missing permissions visible.
- Favor dense, stable inspector patterns over decorative UI.
- Preserve local-first trust: every claim should point back to stored local evidence.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast, visible keyboard focus states, reduced-motion support, and responsive layouts that do not hide core verification data on smaller screens.
